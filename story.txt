Eirik abandoned his home and joined a roaming band of sellswords—ironically, the same group responsible for his father’s death.

He trained under the very man he despised, sharpening his blade and heart with hatred. He swore he would kill the leader, not by surprise, but in a fair duel once he was strong enough.

As the years passed, Eirik became colder, his laughter forgotten. Yet every battle reminded him more of his father’s teachings. Still, he couldn’t forgive. Not yet.
